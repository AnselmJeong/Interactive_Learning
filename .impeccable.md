## Design Context

### Users

Learners use Learnie to study source material with an AI tutor while keeping their place in the main lesson. Saved lookups, explanations, and side chats should remain easy to revisit without interrupting the primary learning flow.

### Brand Personality

Calm, focused, and supportive. The interface should feel like a thoughtful reading companion: editorial rather than dashboard-like, warm rather than clinical, and helpful without demanding attention.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use the existing warm, book-like light and dark themes, restrained teal and blue accents, compact icon actions, and typography-led hierarchy. Saved annotations should resemble marginal learning notes, with clear grouping and little ornamental UI. Avoid noisy controls, oversized buttons, and generic card-dashboard styling.

### Design Principles

- Keep the lesson and source text visually primary; saved enrichments are secondary.
- Use progressive disclosure for long conversations and explanations.
- Prefer compact, recognizable icon actions with accessible names for secondary operations.
- Preserve comfortable reading widths, predictable alignment, and a consistent 4-point spacing rhythm.
- Make saved state and navigation durable and obvious across sessions.
